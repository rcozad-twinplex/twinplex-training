# Module 4: Blanking

## [Page 3]

# Blanking Module Objectives

After completing this module on Blanking, you will be asked to perform the following:

### Objective #1: Blanking Knowledge
Given a list of blank station questions, can correctly answer all of them, without using reference materials.

### Objective #2: Blank Station Components
Given a cross-section of a blank station, can identify each component by name, with 100 percent accuracy.

### Objective #3: Sharpening Blank Station Components
Given any blank punch and die, can sharpen them to the standards found on the performance check, with 100 percent accuracy.

### Objective #4: Mount and Align Blank Tools
Given a blank punch, blank die, and a stripper plate, mount and align them so that the blank has an even break, is held securely by the transfer block, is delivered to the next station without interruption of operation, and is free from defects, with 100 percent accuracy.

### Objective #5: Adjust Roll Feeds
Given a standard roll feed unit, adjust throw so that the bridge between each blank is less than 1/16 of an inch, yet does not cut out, with 100 percent accuracy.

### Objective #6: Correct Blank Station Problems
Given 10 problems in a blank station, can identify the cause of the problem and the possible solutions for each, with 100 percent accuracy.

# Sharpening

The cutting edges of the blank punch (nib) and die must be kept sharp.

**Note 1:** On material that is .015 or thinner, a 15° - 20° angle by 1/16" - 1/8" should be ground on the end of the punch. This helps the thin blank stay in the carrier yoke.

**Note 2:** The die must have a shear angle equal to or greater than one material thickness.

## [Page 8]

## Sharpening a Blank Die

1. Obtain the correct grinding fixture.
2. Clean the die and fixture.
3. Mount the die in the fixture.
4. Mount the fixture on the grinder.
5. Grind the die.
6. Remove the die from the fixture.
7. Remove the burrs from the cutting edge.
8. Clean the tools and store them in the correct location.

**Note:** On a solid blank die, follow the same general procedure.

## Sharpening a Blank Punch

1. Obtain the correct grinding fixture.
2. Clean the punch and fixture.

## [Page 9]

(Sharpening a Blank Punch, continued)

3. Mount the punch in the fixture.
4. Mount the fixture on the grinder.
5. Grind the punch.
6. Remove the burrs from the cutting edge.
7. Clean the tools and store them in the correct location.

**Note:** Grind an angle of approximately 20° by 1/8 inch, perpendicular to the centerline, on material that is .015 or under.

## [Page 10]

# Mounting an ICOP/Platarg Blank Punch

1. Loosen the roller box locking screw and pry the roller box down until it contacts the top of the plunger. Tighten the locking screw.
2. Mount the blank punch correctly.
3. The blank punch must back up into the plunger so that it follows the full-follow cam principle.

(Steps continue through the mounting of the blank punch, observing that the punch backs up into the plunger and follows the full-follow cam.)

## [Page 11]

# Mounting and Aligning an ICOP/Platarg Blank Station

1. Remove the burrs from the tooling and press.
2. Clean the tools and the press.
3. Slide the tools into position.
4. Bar the press down.
5. Insert the parallels.
6. Bar the punch down.
7. Insert the hold-down bolts.
8. Align the tools using the visual method or shims.
9. Double-check the alignment with layout dye, looking for scratches on the nib diameter.

## [Page 12]

(Mounting and Aligning, continued)

10. Mount the stripper plate.
11. Make a blank.
12. Inspect the blank.

After you make and inspect your first blank, it must:

- Be held securely by the transfer block.
- Be free of defects such as nicks, scratches, and burrs.
- Have an even break around its edge.
- Be delivered to the next station without interruption of operation.

**Note:** Non-round blanks may require more than 3 shims to align.

## [Page 17]

# U.S. Baird Sharpening

Sharpening a Baird blank die is similar to the ICOP procedure.

## Sharpening a Baird Blank Punch

1. Obtain the angle plate and two clamps.
2. Clean the angle plate and punch.
3. Set the angle plate on its side on the magnetic chuck.
4. Set the backside of the blank punch against the angle plate.
5. Turn on the magnet and clamp the blank punch to the angle plate.
6. Grind the cutting edge to remove all shear marks and/or chips, using a lapping plate and a medium lapping compound.

**Note:** If the punch is already square, less grinding is required.

## [Page 18]

# U.S. Baird Mount and Align Blank

(Procedure for mounting and aligning a Baird blank and cup station — steps 1 through 17.)

1. Check for and remove burrs from press and tooling.
2. Wipe the press and tools clean of dirt.
3. Assemble the blank punch, cup punch, bracket, and clamps correctly.
4. Slide the blank die retainer assembly onto the press; insert hold-down bolts, leaving them finger-tight.
5. Mount the blank punch assembly correctly.
6. Tighten the bracket bolts to torque specification while holding the blank punch in its fully-up position.
7. Mount the front cup arm dog and tighten the bolt to torque specification.
8. Back off the adjustment screw and hold the punch fully up while snugging up the strap bolts.

## [Page 19]

(U.S. Baird Mount and Align Blank, continued)

When inspecting the blank/cup, it must be identical to the master slide sample, and the cup must be free of wrinkles. Back off the pressure until wrinkles appear, then add just enough pressure to eliminate the wrinkles.

# Adjust Roll Feeds

The components of the roll feed are:

1. Hand-feed extension / hand wheel
2. Feed clutch
3. Feed friction / brake
4. Fine adjustment
5. Crankplate
6. Connecting rod
7. Coarse adjustment
8. Feed rolls
9. Roll pressure adjustments

*[FIGURE: Diagram of a roll feed unit showing the hand-feed extension/hand wheel, feed clutch, feed friction/brake, fine adjustment, crankplate, connecting rod, coarse adjustment, feed rolls, and roll pressure adjustments.]*

## Procedure

1. Jog the press to the correct location to adjust the roll feed.
2. Loosen the lock nut and adjust the arm in the correct direction.
3. Set the bridge to less than 1/16 inch wide, without cutting out.
4. Check the feed rolls for appropriate tension on the stock.

## [Page 23]

(Adjust Roll Feeds, continued)

5. Adjust the roll pressure.
6. Double-check the roll pressure.
7. Check and adjust the chopper.
8. Check and adjust the brake pressure.

**Note:** The bridge must be 1/16 inch or less, yet the material must not cut out.

# Correct Blank Station Problems

The blank station can be the source of several problems. Each of these problems is discussed below. Included with each one is a listing of possible causes and solutions.

| Problem | Possible Causes | Solutions |
|---|---|---|
| **1. Blank is cut out** | a. Bridge too narrow | a. Adjust feed roll for wider bridge |
| | b. Bridge width varies | b. Readjust roll pressure; clean feed clutch; re-adjust feed brake; chopper jammed |
| | c. Chopper jammed | c. Readjust roll pressure so material feeds straight into chopper; adjust chopper to cut at center of blank cut-out; sharpen chopper; provide "shelf" for material to slide along |
| | d. Blank punch does blank | d. Double-check timing of not lift high enough |
| | e. Stock reel problem | e. Alert maintenance; analyze and resolve problem before restarting |
| | f. Material width varies | f. Cut out bad section; replace material |
| **2. Blank produces shavings** | a. Material dragging over sharp corners | a. Break corner on top outside edge of blank die; break corner on front of blank die retainer; remove burrs from bed between blank and cup stations |
