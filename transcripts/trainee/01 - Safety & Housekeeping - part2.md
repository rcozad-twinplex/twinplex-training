# Housekeeping Activities

There are several housekeeping activities which you must perform on a regular basis. They help create a clean environment for the people who follow you on the next shift. Think of these people as your customer - your internal customer. Make it a goal to leave the plant as clean for them as you would for one of our external customers who buy our parts.

Housekeeping activities include:

- [ ] Cabinets, presses, benches, and other tools and equipment are free of oil, grease, and fingerprints.
- [ ] Floors are clean and free of oil, grease, and loose objects.
- [ ] Tooling and press equipment have been returned to their designated spot.
- [ ] Scrap and loose parts have been cleaned from inside the press and dumped in the correct container.
- [ ] Garbage cans have been emptied into the proper hopper and returned to their designated spot.
- [ ] Mop room is clean and all equipment returned to its designated spot.
- [ ] Empty tool carts have been cleaned and returned to their designated spot.
- [ ] All rags have been picked up and placed in appropriate spot.
- [ ] Department is neat and organized, with items in their proper place.

# Housekeeping Assignments

There could also be Housekeeping assignments which must be completed periodically. See your Supervisor for specific assignments.

# Pulling Tools and Cleaning the Press

One of your important tasks is to pull the tools after a job has run out and then to clean the press. Both of these tasks are critical to our goal of cleanliness and organization. They must be done quickly, efficiently, and in an organized way so that the next setup can begin promptly. In fact, pulling and cleaning is considered part of our official measured *"setup time"*. Any lost time increases our setup time, which adds to the cost of our products.

# Pulling and Cleaning Procedures

**Safety Note:** Operating this equipment may require special safety instructions. Operate this equipment only after receiving safety instructions from your Team Leader or Supervisor.

The procedures for pulling tools and cleaning presses are as follows:

1. Remove any scrap from the press area and dump into the proper containers.

2. Measure and record any documentation measurements required for the job that has been completed.

3. Go and get the following equipment and bring each of them to the press:
   - A cart for pulling tools.
   - The power washer.
   - The coolant vacuum.

4. Remove all tooling components from the press. Take care to keep all shims in the proper location. (When there is a shim(s) between a punch and stripper, use wire ties to hold the stripper on to the punch). Use a nylon nut on the threaded end of a chuck to retain any shim that may go between the chuck and plunger. Place tooling on cart - try to keep station components together whenever possible.

5. When all tools, chutes, and components *"unique to the job"* are on the pulling cart, they should be taken to the cleaning station. From here the tools will be cleaned by the utility person and taken to the pre-setup team member that is responsible for the tools.

   All machine components that are related to *"all jobs"* must be cleaned by the person doing the press and returned to the machine for the next setup. I.E. slide friction, die block bolts, etc.

   Before washing the press, turn off the main press power and *"lock out"* the press - the press must be locked out by the person (or by each person) doing the power washing.

6. After tools are at the wash station, go back to the machine. Hook up the power washer and coolant vacuum. The coolant vacuum hose should be placed in the coolant tank to pick up water from spraying the machine, rather than letting it drain onto the floor. Proceed to power wash the press. (On ICOP presses make sure you don't wash the roller boxes unless you plan to spray out the excess water with air and re-oil all roller boxes). Wash all other parts of the machine until excess oil, coolant and scrap are removed. (While cleaning always look for brass shavings indicating a bearing going out and nuts, bolts, etc. that show that something may have broken on the machine during the last production run. If found, notify team leader or toolmaker). After spraying, wipe down only the inside of press, blow out excess water with air, and suck out coolant tank completely. Remove any scrap, slugs or excess water from coolant tank. If next job going in requires a straight oil, the coolant tank must be clean and dry.

7. After unhooking washer and sucker:
   a. Clean and get them ready for the next person.
   b. Coolant sucker must be emptied at the main waste tank.
   c. Return them to their proper location.

   **Note:** All liquids must be disposed of in the holding tank for waste oil.

8. When all this is complete the floor must be mopped and swept up and all things put into their proper place including brooms, wrenches, etc.

9. Clean all windows and wipe down the outside of the guarding.

10. Notify toolmaker or team leader that press is ready for next setup.
