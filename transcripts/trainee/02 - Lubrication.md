# What is a Lubrication System?

The lubrication system on a press is similar to the engine oil in a car. It provides critical lubrication to bearings, moving parts, and wear surfaces.

We all know that the oil level in a car engine must be constantly monitored and maintained. The result of not monitoring this level close enough is thousands of dollars in repair bills and many headaches. The press lubrication system is identical in that it must be monitored consistently. This ensures that the system will effectively lubricate critical bearings and surfaces.

# How does a Lubrication System Work?

A lubrication system has five main components:

- Oil, which provides lubrication.
- A reservoir, which stores the oil.
- A pump, which moves the oil.
- A filter, which filters out the solid impurities in the oil.
- Tubing, which distributes oil to each location.
- Fittings, which control the flow of oil at each location.

The oil used in each press is a specific type recommended by the press manufacturer. This oil is stored in a reservoir, pumped through tubing to each critical location, and forced through a special fitting which controls the amount of oil being dispensed at that location.

# Lubrication Principles

You must remember some key elements about lubrication to perform your job effectively. These include:

- **Know your lubrication system.**
  We have a variety of presses, and for that reason, a variety of lubrication systems as well. While they all perform basically the same function, it is important to know the following for each press lubrication system:
  - Where the reservoir is located.
  - What oil is used.
  - What oil pressure is required.
  - Any special instructions for each system.

# Lubrication Principles - cont.

- **Type of oil varies by press - use the correct oil.**
  Not all presses use the same oil. The press manufacturer recommends what type of oil to use based on the requirements of the press. Some oils provide more lubricity, some more wear resistance, etc. It is critically important to use the recommended oil for the press.

- **Check reservoir level frequently.**
  The lubrication system on a press is designed to use oil. For this reason, the reservoir must be checked often. If the reservoir level remains constant, it could indicate that no oil is getting to critical areas.

- **Monitor oil pressure.**
  The pressure in the lubrication system should remain at a constant 20 - 40 psi. If it falls outside of this range, tell your team leader or supervisor.

- **Monitor the system to prevent problems**
  - Use the correct oil.
  - Maintain the level of oil in the reservoir.
  - Monitor the oil pressure. It should remain at 20 - 40 psi.
  - If the reservoir level remains the same for long periods while the press is running, it could indicate that something is not working as it should.
  - If the oil in the reservoir quickly disappears, it could indicate that there are leaks or other problems in the system.

  **Note:** If you notice anything wrong with the lubrication system, tell your supervisor or team leader immediately, so that it can be corrected.

- **Be careful of contamination.**
  Lubrication systems have filters but they don't handle contamination very well. Typical contaminants include:
  - Other oil - never mix oils together.
  - Dirt and other garbage - always use clean cans to transport oil.
  - The wrong oil - the wrong oil used in a system can be considered a contaminant.

  Always use the right oil, never mix oils, and always use a clean can. Never use a can with other oil in the bottom of it.

- **Clean and return items after use.**
  Always clean each item after use and return it to it's designated spot.

- **Be aware of individual lubrication points.**
  Some presses, like the ICOPS, have sight glass or other types of lubrication points. Be aware of their presence and how often to fill them.

See your supervisor for specific information in which type of oil to use for each situation.
