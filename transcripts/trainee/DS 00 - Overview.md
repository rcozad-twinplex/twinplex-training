# How To Use This Manual

The training method we use in this program is called "*self-paced, self-directed, competency-based training*". To accomplish this method of training, the material you find in each section follows a standard step-by-step process. This process includes:

**Step #1: Theory/knowledge.**

You will learn the basic theory and knowledge required to perform the task.

**Step #2: Written practice.**

Theory is immediately followed by written practice exercises to assist in committing this knowledge to memory.

**Step #3: Demonstration.**

You must ask for a demonstration of the skill if you feel you need one.

**Step #4: Hands-on practice.**

After the demonstration, you will practice each skill several times to become skilled at performing it.

**Step #5: Performance check.**

You will ask for the opportunity to prove your skill by performing one or more competency checks for each task. You must finish each performance check before moving on.

# Stages of Learning

"*First say to yourself what you would be; and then do what you have to do*".
—Epictetus

Every day must be a learning experience to truly "*do what you have to do*." If you understand the stages of learning, you'll have a much better chance of working through early failures. The stages of learning include:

- **Stage One: Awareness** (unconsciously incompetent) — You learn there is a new and better way.
- **Stage Two: Awkwardness** (consciously incompetent) — You'll feel really clumsy at first. It doesn't mean there's something wrong — you just need more experience. Practice, practice, practice.
- **Stage Three: Skillfulness** (consciously competent) — You can perform the skill well, but you need to think about what you're doing.
- **Stage Four: Integration** (unconsciously competent) — You can perform the skill well without thinking about it. The behavior becomes automatic — a part of you.

**Action Plan:**
- Be prepared for the awkwardness.
- Don't give up in the awkwardness stage — persevere through it.
- As you practice you'll get better, more comfortable, and more successful.
- The effort will be worth it!!

# Welcome To The World Of Deep Drawn Metal Stampings

The "*Eyelet*" machine was invented in the late 1800's to produce the eyelets used in shoes. That's where the names "*Eyelet Press*" and "*Eyelet Industry*" came from.

Another name for an "*Eyelet Press*" is the term "*Transfer Press*". A press of this type converts a flat piece of metal into a finished, deep drawn shell. An Eyelet Press has several stations. The flat piece of metal is transferred through each station, each of which performs a different operation. A common series of operations in making a part progresses through stages such as: **Blank, Draw, First Redraw, Second Redraw, Size, Trim, Pierce Bottom, and Lance** — showing a flat disc progressively formed into a deep drawn shell. Each part produced on an Eyelet Press follows a similar progression, but uses its own unique set of tools.

As a **Die Setter**, your role moves beyond operating the press to *setting up* the tools and stations that make these operations happen — preparing for a setup, identifying and inspecting tooling, mounting and adjusting the tools at each station (blanking, cupping, drawing, trimming, piercing), and completing the setup so the press runs quality parts consistently.
