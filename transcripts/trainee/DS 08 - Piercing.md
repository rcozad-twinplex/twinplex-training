# Piercing Components

A piercing station removes material from the bottom of the shell. A piercing station can take on one of two forms:

**Full-bottom Piercing** — removes the entire shell bottom leaving a straight wall on the shell.

**Partial-bottom Piercing** — removes only a portion of the bottom of the shell.

These two are the most often used. In a few cases, you might find a shell with a pierced hole in the flange.

# Sharpening Piercing Station Components

**Sharpening Standards**

**Full bottom piercing**

The punch for a full bottom piercing must have an angle on it to provide a lead in for the punch. A sharp punch would grab the side wall of the shell. The angle must match the radius on the part.

The die is sharp but has a 15° - 20° angle ground on it to provide location for the shell. The cutting edge is still sharp. (See illustration below.)

**Rules of thumb:**

1. The edge of the punch should not scratch your fingernail.
2. If you're unsure of the punch angle, follow these guidelines, in order:
   a. First - match what's already on it.
   b. Second - put what's on the print.
   c. Third - match the radius on the part. See your Supervisor for guidance.

*[FIGURE: Cross-section of a pierce die showing a 1/16" - 1/8" land at the top labeled SHARP, and the 15°-20° ground angle below it.]*

**Partial bottom Piercing**

The punch and die for a partial bottom piercing are sharp.

## [Page 8]

# Sharpening Piercing Station Components - cont.

**Sharpening Procedures**

**Dies**

1. **Setup die in grinder** - set up die on surface grinder, making sure it is blocked up adequately (pierce dies are made of air hard tool steel which does not stick very well to the chuck).
2. **Grind die and demagnetize** - grind top of dial down in multiples of .015 until the sheared areas have been removed. After finishing, demagnetize the die.
3. **Radius outside edge** - set the die up in a lathe and using a dial indicator in the I.D. to true it up. In cases where extreme accuracy is required, the face can also be trued. Radius the outside edge of the die.
4. **Cut angle on die (full bottom pierce dies only)** - full bottom pierce dies only - with a tool bit, turn an angle on the die which is 20° from perpendicular of the center line. This angle should be smooth and straight. It is used to locate the shell in the center of the die. Partial bottom pierce dies are left sharp.
5. **Remove burrs** - with a small stone, hold firmly against the wall of the I.D., remove the burr on the cutting edge.
6. **Reassemble die in block** - reassemble the die into the retainer taking out the appropriate amount of shims from the top of the die and placing them underneath. The top of the die must be flush with the top of the retainer. The shims underneath the die must be flush with the bottom of the retainer.

**Punches**

1. **Set up and grind punch** - set up on surface grinder and remove all shear.
   a. ICOP/Platarg - set up for grinding the punch can take place in one of two ways:
      (1) Using a fixture into which the punch holder is threaded.
      (2) Removal of punch from punch holder and clamping it in a V-block.
   b. U.S. Baird - set up for grinding the punch can take place in one of two ways:
      (1) Setting the whole assembly upright on the back of the punch holder.
      (2) Removal of punch from punch holder and clamping it in a V-block.
2. **Remove burr (partial bottom pierce only)** - using a flat stone, hold firmly against the O.D. of the punch, remove the burr at the cutting edge.
3. **Cut angle or stone radius on cutting edge (full bottom pierce only)** - true up the punch in a lathe. Cut the correct angle or stone a .005 to .010 radius on the cutting edge of the punch. This radius helps the punch enter the part without grabbing and does not interfere with the cutting operation.
4. **Demagnetize and reassemble punch** - demagnetize and reassemble punch in punch holder.

# Mounting and Adjusting Piercing Tools

The procedures for mounting and adjusting piercing tools are as follows:

1. **Clean tools and plunger** - clean shoulder and threads of punch holder and the inside of the plunger of all dirt and foreign material.
2. **Mount punch** - remove stripper arm. Insert the punch into plunger and tighten securely.
3. **Pry punch into die** - jog punch down to within 1/4 inch of die surface and loosen front die. Pry the punch downward until cutting edges are 1/32 of an inch apart. Loosen the chuck and turn the punch into the die without shearing the cutting edges. Pry the punch fully down and then tighten the chuck securely. The punch/lob should still be in the die.
   **Note:** This step is for ICOP and Platarg's with adjustable timing capability.
4. **Align punch and die** - align punch and the either with shims or visually. Tighten front and back die bolts securely. Double-check alignment using layout dye.
5. **Set punch depth** - jog the punch to the fully down position and adjust punch so that it enters about 1/16 of an inch.
6. **Adjust punch timing** - jog press to the point where the fingers are fully back. Loosen the lifter bar and adjust punch to the point where punch cutting edge is in the fully down position. Center the lifter arm on the lifter cam and tighten it securely.
   **Note:** This step is for ICOP and Platarg's with adjustable timing capability.
7. **Mount and adjust stripper** - jog the punch to the top of the stroke. Mount the stripper plate or stripper, leaving it loose. Pry the punch into the stripper opening and jog the punch to the bottom of it's stroke. Using a sample, adjust stripper plate or stripper to strip shell 1/32 off die surface. Center the opening around the punch and tighten securely. Jog one more shell through the station to make final adjustments and double check operation of the station.
8. **Make a shell** - insert a shell and jog the press slowly through one revolution. The punch should begin lifting after the fingers are all the way back.
9. **Inspect shell** - (a) pierced shell is free of score marks, shavings, bulges, and burrs, (b) edge of Piercing has even break, (c) shell is the same length as the slide sample and is held securely by the fingers, (d) shell is delivered to the next station with no interruption of operation.
10. **Double-check all adjustments** - double-check all adjustment and the tightness of all components.

# Correct Piercing Station Problems

The piercing station can be the source of several problems. Each of these problems is discussed below. Included with each one is a listing of possible causes and solutions.

| Problem | Possible Causes | Solutions |
|---|---|---|
| 1. Bulge in side | a. punch catching on part | a. 1) increase radius on side of punch |
| | b. punch shoulder too short | b. 1) grind shoulder |
| | c. punch length too short | c. 1) shim out or replace |
| | d. punch diameter too large | d. 1) check size  2) polish or replace |
| | e. punch radius too small | e. 1) increase radius |
| | f. stripper too long or adjusted too low | f. 1) readjust or replace |
| 2. Shell tips over as slide moves forward | a. shell not fully up | a. 1) re-time punch |
| | b. shell pulling up | b. 1) re-adjust stripper |
| 3. Sheared cutting edges | a. dulled after many hits | a. 1) resharpen |
| | b. part tipped over or pulled up | b. 1) check timing of all stations next to Piercing |
| | c. unknown | c. 1) research and fix before restart |
| 4. Slugs pulling up | a. clearance between punch and die | a. 1) check sizes  2) replace tool(s) |
| | b. die I.D. not choked on heavy parts | b. 1) choke die |
| | c. die land too long | c. 1) cut land back |
| | d. slugs suctioned onto punch | d. 1) vent punch  2) add spring pin |
| | e. die land surface too smooth | e. 1) score die land |
